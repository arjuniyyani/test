Thiruvananthapuram


Thiruvananthapuram District is the southernmost district of Kerala. The district was created in 1949, with its headquarters in the city of Thiruvananthapuram, which is also Kerala's capital. It is also the IT capital of the State.There's so much to discover in Thiruvananthapuram like zoos, historical museums, traditional villages and sparkling beaches that form a perfect backdrop to indulge in an exploration expedition. The Kanakakkunnu Palace, The Napier Museum, Akkulam Lake, Sree Chithra Art Gallery, Science and Technology Museum, Sree Chithra Art Gallery are sighting places, popular for their architectural splendour and historical and religious significance.


Top attractions in Thiruvananthapuram


Padmanabhaswamy Temple

The Padmanabhaswamy temple is a Hindu temple located in Thiruvananthapuram. It is considered as the richest place of worship in the world.Across the world, the Sree Padmanabhaswamy Temple is renowned for its beauty and grandeur.

Kovalam beach

Kovalam is extremely popular among westerners due to shallow waters and low tidal waves.The leisure options at this beach are plenty and diverse. Sunbathing, swimming, herbal body toning massages,cultural programmes and catamaran cruising are some of them.

Ponmudi Hill Station

Flowing through a wilderness area, it has rounded pebbles, cool water, fish, and lush green trees. Some notable tourist attractions located in Ponmudi are Peppara Wildlife Sanctuary, Echo Point and various trekking spots. Mist-laden valleys also draw tourists.

Attukal bhagavathy temple

The Attukal Bhagavathy Temple is a Hindu religious shrine at Attukal in Kerala. Goddess Bhadrakali is the main deity in this temple. "Attukal Pongala" entered into the Guiness Book of World Records for the largest women gathering festival in the world.

Kanakakkunnu Palace

The Kanakakunnu Palace is a beautiful architectural specimen which belonged to the Maharajahs of Travancore.Today the palace and its sprawling grounds are the venue for many cultural meetings and programmes.
